The City of Arts and Innovation

Winston-Salem's Economy Is Built for Growth

Winston-Salem is the fifth-largest city in North Carolina, with a population exceeding 250,000 and a metro area that continues to attract talent, investment, and new businesses every year. Once known primarily for tobacco and textiles, the city has reinvented itself as a hub for healthcare, technology, financial services, and the arts.

The Innovation Quarter -- a 330-acre innovation district in the heart of downtown -- houses more than 200 companies, from biotech startups to established health-tech firms. Wake Forest University and Wake Forest School of Medicine anchor a research corridor that produces cutting-edge science and a steady pipeline of skilled graduates ready to join the local workforce.

Major employers like Wake Forest Baptist Medical Center, Novant Health, Hanesbrands, and the legacy of Reynolds American have shaped the city's economic identity. Truist Financial (formerly BB&T), which was founded right here in Winston-Salem, remains a significant presence downtown. The arts scene -- SECCA, the Stevens Center, and a growing cluster of galleries along Trade Street -- rounds out a city that values creativity as much as commerce.

For businesses operating in this environment, the expectations are high. Customers expect fast responses, seamless digital experiences, and personalized service. That is exactly what automation delivers -- and it is exactly what SystemShift builds for Winston-Salem companies every day.
