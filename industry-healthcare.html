Automated Appointment Reminders

No-shows are the silent revenue killer in every healthcare practice. A single empty chair in a dental office costs $150-$500 depending on the procedure. A missed specialist appointment can cost even more. The industry average no-show rate is 15-20%. That means if you see 30 patients a day, 5-6 of them are not showing up. Every single day. Our multi-channel reminder system attacks this problem systematically. Patients receive a text and email at 7 days out (with a reschedule option), at 2 days out (with a confirmation request), and at 2 hours out (with a final reminder). Each message is personalized with the appointment type, provider name, and office location. Patients can confirm, reschedule, or cancel with a single tap -- no phone call required. When someone cancels, the system automatically offers that slot to patients on your waitlist. The result: no-show rates drop from 15-20% down to 5-8%. For a practice doing $50,000/month in production, that is $5,000-$7,500 in recovered revenue every month. The system pays for itself in the first week.

AI Scheduling Agent

Your front desk is the bottleneck. They are answering phones, checking in patients, verifying insurance, and processing payments -- all at the same time. When the phone rings and they are busy, one of two things happens: the patient waits on hold (and sometimes hangs up), or the call goes to voicemail (and the patient calls another practice). Our AI scheduling agent handles incoming calls 24/7. It answers with your practice name, asks what the patient needs, checks your schedule for available slots that match the appointment type, and books it directly. New patients get routed through an intake workflow. Existing patients are identified and booked into the right provider's schedule. The AI knows that a crown prep needs 90 minutes with Dr. Smith and a hygiene cleaning needs 60 minutes with any hygienist. It handles scheduling complexity that would take your front desk 5 minutes in under 30 seconds. After-hours callers -- who currently get voicemail and call another practice the next morning -- can now book appointments at midnight on a Sunday. Your front desk starts each day with a full schedule they did not have to build.

Digital Patient Intake Forms

The clipboard-and-pen intake process is not just outdated -- it is actively hurting your practice. Patients arrive 15 minutes early, fill out 6 pages of forms with illegible handwriting, and your staff spends another 10 minutes entering that data into your system. That is 25 minutes of wasted time per new patient. And half the forms are incomplete because the patient did not bring their insurance card or did not know their medication dosages. Our digital intake system sends forms via text message 48 hours before the appointment. Patients complete everything on their phone: medical history, current medications, allergies, insurance information (with photo upload of their card), consent forms, and HIPAA acknowledgments. Everything auto-populates into your practice management system through our integration. When the patient arrives, they check in immediately. No clipboard. No waiting. No data entry for your staff. The patient experience starts strong, your data is clean and complete, and your team saves 15-20 minutes per new patient. For a practice seeing 5 new patients a day, that is over an hour of recovered staff time daily.

Patient Reactivation Campaigns

Every dental practice has a database full of patients who have not visited in 6, 12, or 18+ months. These are not lost patients -- they are dormant patients. They liked your practice. They just got busy, forgot, or let their dental health slide. And every month they stay dormant, the odds of them returning drop. Our reactivation system identifies dormant patients and runs personalized outreach campaigns. A patient who last visited 7 months ago gets a friendly reminder: "Hi Sarah, it has been a while since your last visit. We have appointments available next week -- would you like to book your cleaning?" Someone who has been gone 14 months gets a different message with a stronger incentive. The system segments by time since last visit, treatment history, insurance status, and past responsiveness. Each campaign runs 3-4 touchpoints over 2-3 weeks via text and email. Practices typically reactivate 10-15% of dormant patients within the first 90 days. For a practice with 500 dormant patients, that is 50-75 patients returning -- each worth $300-$800 in annual production. That is $15,000-$60,000 in recovered revenue from patients who were already in your database.

Review Management

Patients choose healthcare providers based on Google reviews more than any other factor outside of insurance coverage. A dental practice with 200 five-star reviews will outperform a practice with 30 reviews every time -- even if the second practice does better work. The problem is not quality of care. It is that most practices never ask for reviews in a systematic way. Our review management system sends a text message after every appointment: "Thanks for visiting [Practice Name] today! How was your experience?" Patients who respond positively get a direct link to leave a Google review. Those who have concerns get routed to your office manager for private follow-up. The system sends a follow-up reminder 2 days later for patients who did not respond. We also set up automated responses to new reviews -- both positive and negative -- so every reviewer feels acknowledged. Practices using our review system typically add 20-40 new Google reviews per month, with a marked improvement in their average rating. Higher ratings mean more new patient calls. More calls mean more production. One system drives it all.

Recall & Hygiene Scheduling

Hygiene recall is the lifeblood of any dental practice -- recurring revenue, preventive care, and the gateway to treatment acceptance. But managing recall manually is a nightmare. Your hygienist finishes a cleaning, tells the patient to schedule in 6 months, and the patient nods, walks to the front desk, and says "I will call later." They never call. Six months later, someone on your staff needs to track them down. Our recall system automates the entire cycle. When a hygiene appointment is completed, the system schedules the next appointment and sets up the reminder sequence. If the patient did not pre-schedule, the system sends a booking link at the appropriate interval -- 3 months, 4 months, 6 months, whatever your recall schedule dictates. Patients who do not respond get escalating outreach: text, email, then a staff call trigger. The system also identifies patients who are overdue for perio maintenance, fluoride treatments, or other interval-based services. Hygiene retention rates typically improve 15-25% with automated recall because the patients who "meant to call" actually get reminded and given an easy way to book.
