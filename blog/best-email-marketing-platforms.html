8 Best Email Marketing Platforms for Small Business

By Rock Hunt

Best email marketing platforms for small business compared - real pricing, automation features, and which one fits your budget and goals.

email marketingemail automationsmall business emailmarketing platformsnewsletters
TLDR 8 email platforms compared - real pricing, real pros and cons, no fluff.

- GoHighLevel is the top pick if you want email, SMS, CRM, and funnels in one bill

- Email returns $36 for every $1 spent - but only if your tool doesn't tank deliverability

- Free tiers exist (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, Brevo) but they all cap you fast

You know email marketing works. Every stat says so. $36 back for every $1 spent, according to Litmus. That's a 3,600% return.

But here's where it breaks. You sign up for the wrong platform. You hit a wall at 500 contacts. Your "free plan" suddenly costs $200/month. And your emails land in spam because the platform you picked has garbage deliverability.

59% of consumers say marketing emails influence their purchases (SaleCycle). Segmented campaigns drive 760% more revenue than one-size-fits-all blasts (Campaign Monitor). The channel isn't broken. Your tool might be.

So which platform actually fits a small business budget - without duct-taping five different tools together?

Here's the real breakdown. Real pricing. Real pros and cons. No fluff.

Now let's dig into each one.

1. GoHighLevel - Best All-in-One Platform (Top Pick)

GoHighLevel - $97 to $497/month

This is the one that replaces everything else. Email, SMS, CRM, funnels, landing pages, automation, appointment booking, reputation management - all in one dashboard. Unlimited contacts and unlimited emails on every plan.

Read that again. Unlimited contacts. No penalty for growing your list.

What it does best: Kills the need for 5+ separate tools. You get a full CRM, a funnel builder, email and SMS automation, and a pipeline tracker. One login. One bill.

Pricing:

  • Starter: $97/mo (unlimited contacts, 3 sub-accounts)
  • Unlimited: $297/mo (unlimited everything)
  • SaaS Pro: $497/mo (white-label, sell it as your own)

Pros:

  • Unlimited contacts and emails on all plans
  • Built-in SMS and voice
  • CRM, funnels, and automation included
  • Replaces Mailchimp + Calendly + ClickFunnels + ActiveCampaign

Cons:

  • Steeper learning curve than single-purpose tools
  • $97/mo minimum is higher than email-only platforms
  • Interface can feel overwhelming at first

Best for: Service businesses, agencies, and anyone tired of babysitting 6 different subscriptions. If you want one engine running your entire marketing automation system, this is it.

2. Mailchimp - Best Free Starter

Mailchimp - Free (500 contacts) / $13 to $350/month

The name everyone knows. And for good reason - Mailchimp made email marketing accessible. Drag-and-drop editor, solid templates, and a free plan that lets you test the waters.

What it does best: Gets you sending emails in 20 minutes flat. The template library is massive. The interface is clean. If you've never sent a marketing email before, start here.

Pricing:

  • Free: 500 contacts, 500 sends/month
  • Essentials: $13/mo (500 contacts, 5,000 sends)
  • Standard: $20/mo (500 contacts, 6,000 sends)
  • Premium: $350/mo (10,000 contacts, 150,000 sends)

Pros:

  • Genuinely easy to use
  • Great template library
  • Solid reporting and analytics
  • Integrates with almost everything

Cons:

  • Gets expensive fast as your list grows
  • Free plan is extremely limited now
  • Automation is basic compared to ActiveCampaign
  • Charges for unsubscribed contacts (yes, really)

Best for: Absolute beginners who need to start sending emails today. Just know you'll probably outgrow it within a year.

3. ConvertKit - Best for Creators and Coaches

ConvertKit - Free (1,000 subscribers) / $25 to $50/month

Built specifically for creators. Bloggers, coaches, course sellers, newsletter writers. ConvertKit doesn't try to be everything. It tries to be the best email tool for people who sell knowledge.

What it does best: Visual automation builder that actually makes sense. Tag-based subscriber management (not lists). Built-in landing pages and paid newsletter support. If you sell a course or run a coaching business, this was designed for you.

Pricing:

  • Free: 1,000 subscribers (limited features)
  • Creator: $25/mo (1,000 subs, automation, integrations)
  • Creator Pro: $50/mo (1,000 subs, advanced reporting, referral system)

Pros:

  • Tag-based system is cleaner than list-based
  • Visual automations are intuitive
  • Built-in commerce for digital products
  • Free plan supports 1,000 subscribers

Cons:

  • Limited design options for emails (intentionally plain)
  • No built-in CRM
  • Reporting is basic on lower plans
  • Not ideal for e-commerce businesses

Best for: Solopreneurs, coaches, course creators, and newsletter writers. If your business runs on content and expertise, ConvertKit gets out of your way and lets you build.

4. ActiveCampaign - Best for Advanced Automation

ActiveCampaign - $15 to $145/month

This is where you go when Mailchimp's automations feel like training wheels. ActiveCampaign has the most powerful automation builder on this list. Period.

What it does best: Complex automation workflows. Site tracking. Lead scoring. Conditional logic that goes 10 layers deep. If you want to send the right email to the right person at the right moment, ActiveCampaign gives you the controls.

Pricing:

  • Starter: $15/mo (1,000 contacts, basic automation)
  • Plus: $49/mo (1,000 contacts, CRM, landing pages)
  • Pro: $79/mo (1,000 contacts, predictive sending, split automation)
  • Enterprise: $145/mo (advanced features, custom reporting)

Pros:

  • Best-in-class automation builder
  • Built-in CRM on Plus plan and up
  • Site tracking shows you what contacts do on your website
  • Predictive sending and content

Cons:

  • Learning curve is real
  • CRM is not as robust as dedicated CRM tools
  • Gets pricey as contacts grow
  • Lite plan has limited features

Best for: Businesses that have outgrown Mailchimp and need serious automation. If you want to build workflows that automate your entire follow-up system, this is the tool.

5. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) - Best Budget Option with SMS

Brevo - Free (300 emails/day) / $25 to $65/month

Brevo charges by emails sent, not contacts stored. That's a game-changer if you have a big list but don't email every day. Plus it includes SMS and WhatsApp messaging built right in.

What it does best: Multi-channel on a budget. Email, SMS, and WhatsApp from one dashboard. The free plan gives you 300 emails per day with unlimited contacts. Try finding that anywhere else.

Pricing:

  • Free: 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts
  • Starter: $25/mo (20,000 emails/month)
  • Business: $65/mo (20,000 emails, marketing automation, A/B testing)
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

Pros:

  • Pricing based on emails sent, not list size
  • SMS and WhatsApp included
  • Unlimited contacts on every plan
  • Solid transactional email features

Cons:

  • Free plan includes Brevo branding
  • Automation builder isn't as polished as ActiveCampaign
  • Fewer integrations than Mailchimp
  • Template selection is smaller

Best for: Budget-conscious businesses that want email plus SMS without paying for two platforms. Also strong for transactional emails (order confirmations, shipping updates).

6. Constant Contact - Best for Events and Nonprofits

Constant Contact - $12 to $80/month

Been around since 1995. Not flashy. Not trendy. But rock-solid for what it does - simple email marketing with built-in event management tools.

What it does best: Event invitations, registrations, and follow-ups. Social media posting. Simple email campaigns that don't require a marketing degree to set up. If you run events, workshops, or fundraisers, this is purpose-built for you.

Pricing:

  • Lite: $12/mo (500 contacts, basic email)
  • Standard: $35/mo (500 contacts, automation, A/B testing)
  • Premium: $80/mo (500 contacts, advanced automation, SEO tools)

Pros:

  • Event management tools built in
  • Excellent phone support (rare in this space)
  • High deliverability rates
  • Nonprofit discounts available

Cons:

  • Automation is basic
  • Templates feel dated compared to competitors
  • Limited A/B testing on lower plans
  • No free plan

Best for: Local businesses, nonprofits, churches, community organizations, and anyone who runs regular events. Simple and reliable.

7. GetResponse - Best for Webinar Marketing

GetResponse - Free (500 contacts) / $13.30 to $97.58/month

GetResponse is the only platform on this list with built-in webinar hosting. If webinars are part of your sales funnel, you just eliminated the need for Zoom or WebinarJam.

What it does best: Combines email marketing with webinars, funnels, and automation in one platform. The webinar tool supports up to 1,000 attendees. Run the webinar, capture leads, and nurture them - all without leaving GetResponse.

Pricing:

  • Free: 500 contacts, basic email
  • Email Marketing: $13.30/mo (1,000 contacts, autoresponders)
  • Marketing Automation: $41.30/mo (1,000 contacts, automation, webinars)
  • Ecommerce Marketing: $97.58/mo (1,000 contacts, transactional emails, e-commerce tools)

Pros:

  • Built-in webinar hosting (unique feature)
  • Conversion funnels included
  • Good automation builder
  • Competitive pricing

Cons:

  • Webinars only on mid-tier and up
  • Deliverability can be inconsistent
  • Interface feels cluttered in places
  • Free plan is very limited

Best for: Businesses that use webinars to generate and convert leads. Coaches, consultants, and educators who want their email tool and webinar tool in the same place.

8. AWeber - Best for Simplicity and Deliverability

AWeber - Free (500 subscribers) / $15 to $900/month

AWeber has been in the email game since 1998. That's 28 years of building relationships with inbox providers. The result? Some of the best deliverability rates in the industry.

What it does best: Gets your emails into inboxes. Not spam folders. Not promotions tabs. Inboxes. If deliverability is your top concern, AWeber's track record speaks for itself.

Pricing:

  • Free: 500 subscribers, 3,000 sends/month
  • Lite: $15/mo (unlimited subscribers)
  • Plus: $30/mo (advanced automation, split testing)
  • Unlimited: $900/mo (priority support, full features)

Pros:

  • Excellent deliverability rates
  • Been around since 1998 (stability matters)
  • Solid autoresponder sequences
  • Free migration from other platforms

Cons:

  • Automation is basic compared to ActiveCampaign
  • Design options are limited
  • Reporting could be deeper
  • Unlimited plan is wildly overpriced

Best for: Email purists who care about one thing above all else - making sure their emails actually get read. Also great for bloggers and small list owners who want something reliable and straightforward.

So Which One Should You Pick?

Here's the honest answer. It depends on what's broken in your business right now.

If you're drowning in tools and paying for a CRM, a funnel builder, an email platform, and a scheduling tool separately - GoHighLevel replaces all of it. One system. One bill. That's the play for service businesses who want to stop babysitting disconnected software.

If you're just getting started and need to send your first email campaign today - Mailchimp or AWeber. Low barrier. Quick results. You can always migrate later.

If you sell courses or coaching - ConvertKit. It was built for your exact business model.

If your automations need to be surgical - ActiveCampaign. Nothing else comes close on workflow complexity. When you're ready to build automated email sequences that respond to behavior in real time, this is the tool.

If you're budget-conscious and want SMS too - Brevo. Pay for emails sent, not contacts stored. Smart pricing model.

The Real Secret? The Platform Isn't the System

Here's what most people get wrong. They obsess over which platform to pick but never build the system around it.

An email platform without a lead generation strategy is a megaphone in an empty room. You need traffic flowing in. You need landing pages converting. You need a CRM tracking every interaction. You need follow-up sequences that fire without you touching anything.

The platform is the engine. But an engine sitting on a garage floor doesn't go anywhere. You need the whole machine.

Pick a platform from this list. Set it up. Then build the system that feeds it, tracks it, and scales it.

That's how email marketing stops being a chore and starts being a revenue channel that runs while you sleep.
