7 Best Landing Page Builders for Small Business

By Rock Hunt

Best landing page builders for small business compared - pricing, templates, and conversion features. From free tools to all-in-one platforms.

landing pageslanding page buildersmall businesslead generationfunnel builder
TLDR 7 landing page builders ranked by what matters for small business - not enterprise fluff.

- GoHighLevel wins if you want everything in one platform (CRM, email, SMS, funnels)

- Carrd is dirt cheap at $9/yr if you just need a simple page

- More landing pages = more leads. Businesses with 30+ pages generate 7x more leads

You're paying for ads. Running campaigns. Posting content. But where does all that traffic actually go?

If the answer is "my homepage" - you're leaking leads. A homepage tries to be everything to everyone. A landing page does one job. And businesses with 30+ landing pages generate 7x more leads than those with fewer than 10 (HubSpot).

That's not a small difference. That's the difference between a business that scales and one that stays stuck babysitting every lead manually.

The average landing page conversion rate sits at 5.89% (Unbounce). But most small businesses are nowhere close to that because they're using the wrong tools - or no tools at all. Duct-taped WordPress pages. Generic templates that look like every other site on the internet.

Here are the 7 best landing page builders for small business, ranked by what actually matters: ease of use, conversion features, and whether the pricing makes sense for a business that isn't burning VC money.

1. GoHighLevel - Best All-in-One Platform

GoHighLevel - $97 to $497/mo

If you're tired of duct-taping 6 different tools together, this is the one. GoHighLevel isn't just a landing page builder. It's your CRM, your funnel builder, your email and SMS automation, your booking system, and your landing pages - all in one platform.

The drag-and-drop builder comes with 100+ templates. But the real power is what happens after someone fills out your form. The lead goes straight into your CRM. An automated follow-up fires instantly. A text message hits their phone in under 60 seconds.

No Zapier. No broken integrations. No praying that your landing page tool talks to your email tool.

Pricing:

  • Starter: $97/mo
  • Unlimited: $297/mo
  • SaaS Pro: $497/mo

Pros:

  • Landing pages, CRM, email, SMS, funnels, and booking all in one place
  • Built-in speed-to-lead automation - follow up in seconds, not hours
  • White-label option for agencies
  • No per-lead or per-contact charges on Unlimited plan

Cons:

  • Steeper learning curve than single-purpose tools
  • $97/mo starting price is higher than standalone builders
  • Template designs aren't as polished as Unbounce or Instapage

Best for: Service businesses, agencies, and anyone who wants to stop paying for 5 separate tools. If you need a CRM anyway, this eliminates the stack entirely.

2. Unbounce - Best for A/B Testing

Unbounce - $74 to $187/mo

Unbounce built its reputation on one thing: helping you test your way to higher conversions. Their Smart Traffic feature uses AI to automatically route visitors to the landing page variant most likely to convert them.

Not a generic "we have AI" claim. Smart Traffic analyzes visitor attributes - device, location, time of day - and sends each person to the version that performs best for people like them. Unbounce reports a 30% average conversion lift from Smart Traffic alone.

The builder itself is solid. True drag-and-drop with pixel-level control. You're not locked into rigid templates. You can move elements wherever you want.

Pricing:

  • Build: $74/mo (up to 20,000 visitors)
  • Experiment: $112/mo (up to 30,000 visitors)
  • Optimize: $187/mo (up to 50,000 visitors)

Pros:

  • Smart Traffic AI for automatic optimization
  • True pixel-perfect drag-and-drop editor
  • Dynamic text replacement for PPC campaigns
  • 100+ high-converting templates

Cons:

  • No built-in CRM or email automation
  • Visitor limits on every plan - you pay more as you grow
  • No free plan

Best for: Marketers and businesses running paid ads who want to squeeze every conversion out of their traffic. If you test headlines, offers, and layouts obsessively, this is your tool.

3. Leadpages - Best Budget Option

Leadpages - $37 to $74/mo

Leadpages does one thing well: it makes building landing pages fast and cheap. At $37/mo, it's the most affordable serious landing page builder on this list. And you get unlimited landing pages, unlimited traffic, and unlimited leads on every plan.

No per-visitor pricing. No lead caps. You pay one price and build as many pages as you want. For a small business watching every dollar, that matters.

The template library has 200+ designs sorted by conversion rate. Pick a high-performing template, swap in your content, publish. You can have a page live in under an hour.

Pricing:

  • Standard: $37/mo
  • Pro: $74/mo

Pros:

  • Unlimited pages, traffic, and leads on all plans
  • 200+ templates sorted by conversion rate
  • Built-in payment acceptance (sell directly from landing pages)
  • Alert bars and pop-ups included

Cons:

  • A/B testing only on Pro plan ($74/mo)
  • Editor is less flexible than Unbounce or Instapage
  • No CRM or automation built in

Best for: Solopreneurs and small businesses who need solid landing pages without spending $100+/mo. If your budget is tight but you know you need better pages than what your website builder offers, start here.

4. Instapage - Best for Ad Campaigns

Instapage - $79 to $199/mo

Instapage was built for one specific use case: making your paid ads convert better. Their 1:1 ad-to-page personalization means every ad group gets its own tailored landing page experience.

Running Google Ads for 5 different services? Instapage lets you create 5 unique post-click experiences that match the exact ad the visitor clicked. The messaging stays consistent from ad to page. No confusion. No generic "welcome to our site" nonsense.

The built-in heatmaps show you exactly where visitors click, scroll, and drop off. You don't need a separate analytics tool to figure out what's broken.

Pricing:

  • Building: $79/mo
  • Converting: $199/mo

Pros:

  • 1:1 ad-to-page personalization
  • Built-in heatmaps and analytics
  • Collaboration tools for teams (commenting, approval workflows)
  • 500+ templates with industry-specific designs

Cons:

  • Conversion limits on Building plan
  • Higher price for full feature access
  • Can feel complex for simple use cases

Best for: PPC-heavy businesses spending real money on Google Ads or Meta Ads. If you're dropping $2,000+/mo on ads and sending traffic to generic pages, Instapage pays for itself fast. Pair it with an automated sales funnel and you stop leaving money on the table.

5. Carrd - Best Ultra-Cheap Option

Carrd - Free / $9 to $49/yr

Notice that price. Per year. Not per month.

Carrd is dead simple. You build one-page sites. No complex funnels. No multi-step forms. Just clean, fast, single-page landing pages for almost nothing.

The free plan gives you 3 sites. The Pro Standard plan at $19/yr gives you 10 sites with custom domains, forms, and embeds. That's less than what most tools charge for a single month.

The trade-off? Limited functionality. No A/B testing. No heatmaps. No automation. But if you need a simple lead capture page or a link-in-bio page, Carrd gets it done for pocket change.

Pricing:

  • Free: 3 sites, carrd.co subdomain
  • Pro Lite: $9/yr (3 sites, custom domains)
  • Pro Standard: $19/yr (10 sites, forms, embeds)
  • Pro Plus: $49/yr (25 sites, widgets, analytics)

Pros:

  • Insanely cheap - under $5/mo even on the top plan
  • Beautiful, minimal templates
  • Fast load times
  • Zero learning curve

Cons:

  • One-page sites only - no multi-step funnels
  • No A/B testing or analytics (except basic on Pro Plus)
  • Limited integrations
  • Not built for serious lead generation at scale

Best for: Side projects, simple lead capture pages, personal sites, and anyone who needs something live in 20 minutes for almost no money. Not a replacement for a real landing page builder if you're running serious campaigns.

6. ClickFunnels - Best for Sales Funnels

ClickFunnels - $97 to $297/mo

ClickFunnels isn't really a landing page builder. It's a funnel builder. The difference matters.

A landing page captures a lead. A funnel takes that lead through a multi-step journey - opt-in page, thank you page, upsell page, order form, one-click upsell, confirmation. ClickFunnels was built to handle that entire sequence.

If you're selling courses, coaching programs, or digital products, ClickFunnels gives you the complete sales machine. Order bumps, upsells, downsells, membership areas - it's all built in.

Pricing:

  • Startup: $97/mo
  • Pro: $297/mo

Pros:

  • Complete sales funnel builder, not just landing pages
  • Built-in order forms, upsells, and payment processing
  • Membership site functionality
  • Large template and funnel marketplace

Cons:

  • Expensive for just landing pages
  • Page editor feels dated compared to newer tools
  • Can be slow and buggy under heavy traffic
  • Steep learning curve for the full platform

Best for: Course creators, coaches, consultants, and anyone selling digital products through multi-step funnels. If you just need landing pages, this is overkill. If you need a complete sales system, it's purpose-built for that.

7. Swipe Pages - Best for Mobile-First

Swipe Pages - $29 to $59/mo

Here's a stat that should bother you: over 60% of web traffic is mobile. But most landing page builders design for desktop first, then hope the mobile version doesn't look broken.

Swipe Pages flips that. It builds AMP (Accelerated Mobile Pages) landing pages that load in under a second on mobile devices. Not 3 seconds. Not 5 seconds. Under one second.

Page speed directly affects conversions. Google found that as page load time goes from 1 second to 3 seconds, bounce probability increases by 32%. Swipe Pages eliminates that problem entirely.

Pricing:

  • Startup: $29/mo
  • Marketer: $59/mo
  • Agency: $119/mo

Pros:

  • AMP pages load in under 1 second on mobile
  • A/B testing included on all plans
  • Clean, modern editor
  • Affordable starting price

Cons:

  • Smaller template library than competitors
  • AMP has some design limitations
  • Fewer integrations than Unbounce or Instapage
  • Less well-known (smaller community and support resources)

Best for: Businesses where most traffic comes from mobile - local service businesses, social media-driven brands, and anyone running Instagram or TikTok ads. If your analytics show 60%+ mobile traffic and your current pages load slowly, Swipe Pages fixes that fast.

How to Pick the Right Builder

Stop comparing feature lists. Start with your actual situation.

You need everything in one place. You don't want to manage a landing page tool, a CRM, an email tool, and an SMS tool separately. You want one login. GoHighLevel. Done.

You're running paid ads and need to test. Your ad spend is significant and you need to know which page variant converts best. Unbounce or Instapage.

You're on a tight budget. Every dollar counts. You need professional pages without a $100/mo commitment. Leadpages or Carrd.

You're selling courses or digital products. You need funnels, not just pages. Order bumps, upsells, the whole machine. ClickFunnels.

Your traffic is mostly mobile. Your pages load slowly on phones and you're losing visitors before they even see your offer. Swipe Pages.

The Landing Page Itself Matters More Than the Tool

Here's the uncomfortable truth. The best landing page builder in the world won't save a bad page.

A garbage headline on Instapage converts the same as a garbage headline on Carrd. Zero.

The tool gives you the canvas. You still need to build the page correctly. The right headline. A clear offer. One CTA. Social proof. Fast load time.

If you haven't nailed those fundamentals, no tool fixes that. Get the basics right first. Then pick the builder that fits your budget and workflow.

Stop Fire-Fighting Your Tech Stack

Most small businesses aren't struggling because they picked the wrong landing page builder. They're struggling because their landing pages don't connect to anything.

The page captures a lead. Then what? Does that lead sit in a spreadsheet? Do you remember to follow up 3 days later? Does anyone call them?

The real bottleneck isn't the landing page. It's the system behind it. Your pages need to feed into a CRM that actually works, trigger automated follow-up, and move leads through your funnel without you babysitting every step.

That's what separates businesses that collect leads from businesses that convert them.

Pick a builder. Build a page. But build the system behind it too. That's what scales.
