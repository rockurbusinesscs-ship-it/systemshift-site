7 GoHighLevel Automation Recipes That Save 20 Hours a Week

By Rock Hunt

GoHighLevel automation recipes that save 20 hours weekly. Speed-to-lead, missed call text-back, review requests, and 4 more workflows you can steal today.

GoHighLevelautomationworkflowsCRM

Your best leads are dying in your inbox right now. Not because you do not care. Because you are too busy to respond in time.

The Real Cost of Manual Follow-Up

You get a form submission at 2:14 PM. You are on a job site. Or in a meeting. Or eating lunch for the first time today. By the time you see it at 5:30 PM, that lead has already called two of your competitors.

Research from Harvard Business Review found that responding within five minutes makes you 100 times more likely to connect with a lead. Five minutes. Not five hours.

Now multiply that across every lead, every missed call, every stale contact, every client who forgot to leave a review. The math is brutal. You are leaving money on the table every single day because there are not enough hours to manually chase every opportunity.

This is not a discipline problem. It is a systems problem. And GoHighLevel solves it with automation workflows you set up once and never think about again.

Here Are 7 Recipes You Can Build Today

1. Speed-to-Lead Auto-Response

Trigger: New contact created via form, ad, or landing page.

Workflow: Immediately send an SMS saying "Hey [first name], thanks for reaching out. Quick question - what is the best time to chat today?" Wait 2 minutes. Send an email with more detail about your services. Wait 5 minutes. If no reply, trigger an internal notification to your sales rep with the lead's info.

Why it works: The lead gets a response in under 10 seconds. They feel seen. Your competitor is still checking voicemail.

Time saved: 3 hours a week minimum.

2. Missed Call Text-Back

Trigger: Inbound call goes unanswered.

Workflow: Immediately send an SMS: "Sorry we missed your call. How can we help? Reply here or book a time that works: [calendar link]." If they reply, route the conversation to your inbox. If they book, send a confirmation with appointment details.

Why it works: 80% of callers who hit voicemail never call back. But they will text back. This catches the leads you would have lost.

Time saved: 2 hours a week.

3. Automated Review Request Sequence

Trigger: Opportunity moved to "Completed" or "Closed Won" pipeline stage.

Workflow: Wait 2 hours. Send SMS: "Hey [first name], thanks for choosing us. If you had a great experience, would you mind leaving a quick Google review? [review link]." Wait 3 days. If no review detected, send a follow-up email with the same link and a note about how much reviews help small businesses.

Why it works: People intend to leave reviews. They just forget. A well-timed nudge right after the service - when satisfaction is highest - converts at 30% or higher.

Time saved: 3 hours a week.

4. Appointment Reminder Sequence

Trigger: Appointment booked on GoHighLevel calendar.

Workflow: Immediately send confirmation email with date, time, address, and what to expect. 24 hours before: send SMS reminder. 1 hour before: send final SMS with address or meeting link. If appointment is cancelled, trigger a rebooking workflow with alternative times.

Why it works: No-shows kill service businesses. This sequence cuts no-show rates by 40-60%. That is real revenue recovered.

Time saved: 2 hours a week.

5. Stale Lead Reactivation

Trigger: Contact has had no activity for 90 days AND pipeline stage is not "Closed Won" or "Lost."

Workflow: Send SMS: "Hey [first name], we connected a while back about [service]. Still something you are thinking about? Happy to pick up where we left off." Wait 2 days. If no reply, send email with a relevant case study or seasonal offer. Wait 5 days. If still no reply, send final SMS with a specific limited-time offer or free consultation.

Why it works: These leads already raised their hand once. They know you. Reactivating 5% of your dead leads costs nothing compared to acquiring new ones. Most businesses never follow up past the first attempt.

Time saved: 4 hours a week.

6. Birthday and Anniversary Campaigns

Trigger: Custom date field matches current date (birthday, contract anniversary, or project completion date).

Workflow: Send personalized SMS: "Happy birthday [first name]. Thanks for being part of the [business name] family." For anniversaries: send email with a loyalty discount or service credit. Optional: trigger a task for your team to make a personal phone call for VIP clients.

Why it works: This is the easiest way to stay top of mind. When someone needs your service again, they call the company that remembered their birthday. Not the one that ghosted them after the invoice cleared.

Time saved: 1 hour a week.

7. Invoice Follow-Up Sequence

Trigger: Invoice sent (integrated with Stripe or QuickBooks).

Workflow: Wait 3 days. If unpaid, send friendly SMS reminder: "Hey [first name], just a heads up - invoice #[number] is still open. Here is the payment link: [link]." Wait 4 more days. Send email with invoice attached and a note about late fees. Wait 7 more days. Trigger internal task for personal outreach.

Why it works: Most late payments are not malicious. People are busy and forget. Automated reminders recover 60-70% of overdue invoices without a single awkward phone call.

Time saved: 5 hours a week.

The Math

Add it up. Speed-to-lead saves 3 hours. Missed call text-back saves 2. Reviews save 3. Appointment reminders save 2. Stale leads save 4. Birthday campaigns save 1. Invoice follow-up saves 5.

That is 20 hours a week. Half a full-time employee. Running 24/7 without sick days, vacations, or bad moods.

What To Do Right Now

Pick one. Just one recipe from this list. The one that made you think "yeah, we lose money on that." Build it today. Run it for two weeks. Measure the difference.

Then build the next one.
