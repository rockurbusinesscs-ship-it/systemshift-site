## You're Not Too Small to Automate. You're Too Small Not To.

Here's the lie small business owners tell themselves:

"Automation is for big companies. I don't have the budget. I don't have the team. I'll get to it later."

Later never comes. And while you're doing everything manually, your competitor with the same size team is running circles around you.

They're not working harder. They have systems running in the background doing the work for them.

Here are the five automations you need. Today. Not next quarter.

## 1. Welcome Sequence for New Leads

When someone fills out a form, downloads something, or reaches out for the first time - what happens?

If the answer is "I get a notification and follow up when I can," you're losing money.

**The automation:**
- Lead fills out form
- Instant text: "Hey [name], got your request. Here's what happens next..."
- Email 1 (immediate): Welcome + what to expect
- Email 2 (Day 2): Your best case study or testimonial
- Email 3 (Day 4): FAQ answers before they ask
- Email 4 (Day 7): Direct CTA to book a call

This runs for every single lead. No exceptions. No "I forgot." No "I was busy."

**Result:** Businesses that send a welcome sequence see **33% higher long-term engagement** than those who don't.

## 2. Review Request System

You have happy customers. They just never leave reviews because nobody asks.

Or you ask once, in person, and they say "sure!" and then forget.

**The automation:**
- Job completed or service delivered (trigger)
- Day 1: Text message with direct Google Review link
- Day 3: If no review, follow-up email with the link again
- Day 7: Final friendly nudge via text

Three touches. Completely automated. We've seen clients go from **12 reviews to 80+ in six months** just from this one sequence.

Reviews are SEO fuel. They're also trust signals for every potential customer who Googles you before calling.

## 3. Re-Engagement Campaign

You have a list of old leads and past customers sitting in your CRM doing nothing.

They already know you. They already trusted you enough to reach out once. And you're out here spending money on new leads while ignoring the goldmine in your database.

**The automation:**
- Every 90 days, segment contacts who haven't engaged
- Send a "checking in" email with a new offer or update
- Follow up with a text for those who open but don't click
- Route hot responses to your sales pipeline

**One client reactivated 23 old leads** from a single re-engagement campaign. Seven of them became paying customers. From people who were already in the database.

Stop paying for new leads when you haven't worked the ones you already have.

## 4. Appointment Reminder Sequence

No-shows kill service businesses. The average no-show rate is **20-30%**. That's one out of every four or five appointments just gone.

**The automation:**
- Appointment booked (trigger)
- Immediate: Confirmation text + email with details
- 24 hours before: Reminder text with reschedule option
- 2 hours before: Final reminder with directions/link
- After appointment: Follow-up text asking about their experience

This cuts no-show rates by **50-80%**. We're talking about real revenue that was walking out the door because of a simple reminder system nobody bothered to build.

## 5. Referral Request System

Your best marketing channel is word of mouth. But you're leaving it to chance.

Nobody wakes up and thinks "I should refer my contractor to someone today." They need a prompt. A reason. A system.

**The automation:**
- 7 days after successful service delivery (trigger)
- Text: "Hey [name], glad we could help. Know anyone else who could use [service]? We'd love to take care of them too."
- Include a referral link that tracks who sent them
- When someone uses the link, notify the referrer and your team
- Optional: Offer a referral incentive (discount, gift card, credit)

Most businesses never ask for referrals systematically. The ones that do see **15-25% of new business** come from referral automations alone.
