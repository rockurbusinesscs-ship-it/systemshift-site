What We Build for Home Service Companies

Home Services Automation That Fills Your Schedule

Built for the realities of running a trades business. Your techs are on ladders, under houses, and on rooftops. Your office might be a truck dashboard. These systems work while your hands are full.

📞

AI Phone Answering

Your phone rings while your crew is knee-deep in a furnace install. Nobody answers. That homeowner calls the next company on Google. Job lost. Revenue gone. This happens dozens of times per month for the average home services company. Our AI voice agent answers every call -- 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. It sounds like a real person on your team. It gathers the caller's name, address, describes the issue they are having, assesses urgency, and books a service appointment directly on your schedule. Emergency calls -- no heat in January, gas leak, flooding -- get immediately routed to your on-call technician with all the caller's details. Routine calls get booked into your normal schedule with the right time slot for the job type. The AI knows that a water heater replacement needs a 3-hour block and a thermostat install needs 1 hour. No more missed calls. No more voicemail black holes. No more lost revenue because you were too busy working to answer the phone.

🚚

Dispatch Automation

Managing a team of technicians across multiple job sites is chaos without a system. Who is closest to the next call? Who has the right skills for this repair? Who is running behind schedule? Our dispatch automation integrates with your field service platform -- ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, or FieldEdge -- and helps optimize your routing. When a new job comes in, the system identifies which technician is closest, has the right certification, and has availability. Customers receive automated appointment confirmations with their technician's name and photo, an estimated arrival window, and a text message when the tech is en route. Your office manager stops playing dispatcher and starts focusing on customer service and growth. For multi-truck operations, this alone can add 1-2 extra jobs per day per truck through better routing and reduced downtime between calls.

🌞

Seasonal Campaigns

Every HVAC company knows the seasonal grind: scrambling in summer and winter, slow in spring and fall. But the best companies smooth that curve with proactive seasonal marketing. Our system runs it on autopilot. In March, your database gets AC tune-up offers before the heat hits. In September, furnace maintenance campaigns go out before the first cold snap. During slow seasons, the system pushes maintenance agreement sign-ups, indoor air quality promotions, and duct cleaning specials. Each campaign is segmented -- homeowners who bought a new system last year get different messaging than homeowners with 15-year-old equipment. The system tracks which offers each customer has received, what they have responded to, and adjusts future campaigns based on their behavior. You set the strategy once. The system executes it every season, every year, automatically. No more scrambling to put together a last-minute email blast when the phone stops ringing.

🔧

Maintenance Reminders

Your maintenance agreement customers are your most valuable asset -- recurring revenue, predictable scheduling, and first dibs on upsells. But they only stay valuable if they actually schedule their maintenance visits. Our system tracks every customer's service history and automatically sends personalized reminders when their next maintenance is due. "Hi John, your annual furnace tune-up is due next month. Your last service was November 2025. Book your appointment here -- agreement members get priority scheduling." One tap to book. No phone call required. If they do not respond, a follow-up goes out one week later. Then a final reminder two weeks after that. For customers without agreements, the maintenance reminder doubles as a sales touchpoint -- "Your AC system is due for a tune-up. Did you know our maintenance agreement includes 2 annual tune-ups, priority emergency service, and 15% off repairs? Learn more here." Maintenance agreement retention rates typically improve 20-30% with automated reminders because the friction of scheduling disappears.

⭐

Review Collection Engine

In home services, your Google review count and rating directly determines how many calls you get. Homeowners check reviews before they call. Period. But most HVAC companies and plumbers have 30-50 reviews when they should have 300-500. The problem is not service quality -- it is that nobody asks consistently. Our review engine sends an automated text message within 2 hours of every completed service call: "Thanks for choosing [Your Company]! How was your experience with [Technician Name] today?" Happy customers get a direct Google review link. The system handles the follow-up if they do not respond -- a gentle reminder 2 days later, then 5 days later. We also set up automated responses to new Google reviews so every review gets acknowledged promptly. Companies using our review engine typically add 15-25 new Google reviews per month, with an average rating improvement of 0.2-0.4 stars within 6 months. That translates directly to more calls, more jobs, and more revenue.

💰

Estimate Follow-Up

You sent 20 estimates last week. How many have you followed up on? If you are like most home services companies, the answer is "a few" -- the big ones, the ones you remembered, the ones where the customer called back. The other 15 are sitting in limbo, and half of them will go to a competitor who followed up first. Our estimate follow-up system tracks every pending estimate and runs an automated sequence: a thank-you text within an hour of the estimate, a follow-up email 2 days later with the estimate attached, a check-in call trigger at 5 days, and a final "is this still something you are considering?" message at 10 days. The system also notifies you when a prospect opens the estimate email, so you know exactly who is shopping and when to call. Home services companies using automated estimate follow-up close 15-25% more jobs from the same number of estimates -- that is pure revenue growth with zero additional marketing spend.

Frequently Asked Questions

Home Services Automation FAQ

How does AI phone answering work for HVAC companies?
Our AI voice agent answers your phone 24/7 -- including nights, weekends, and holidays when emergencies happen most. It sounds natural, gathers the caller's information (name, address, issue description, urgency level), and either books a service appointment or routes emergency calls to your on-call technician. Most callers cannot tell they are speaking with AI. You never miss a call again, even when your entire crew is on job sites.
Can automation help with seasonal demand spikes in HVAC?
Absolutely. We build seasonal campaign automations that ramp up marketing before peak seasons -- AC tune-up campaigns in spring, furnace maintenance in fall. The system sends targeted offers to your existing customer database, runs re-engagement sequences for dormant customers, and manages the surge in inbound calls and bookings without overwhelming your office staff. During slow seasons, the system runs maintenance agreement campaigns to stabilize revenue.
How much does home services automation cost?
Project-based builds start at $500 with a typical range of $500 to $5,000+. A basic AI phone answering and booking system might be $500-$2,000, while a full home services automation suite with dispatch, seasonal campaigns, maintenance reminders, and review collection runs $3,000-$5,000+. Monthly retainers start at $500/month. Every project includes a free systems audit, 30-day testing period, and training.
Will the AI know the difference between an emergency call and a routine service request?
Yes. We train the AI to recognize emergency keywords and situations -- no heat in winter, gas smell, water flooding, electrical sparks, carbon monoxide alarm. Emergency calls get immediately routed to your on-call technician with all the caller's information. Routine requests get booked into your normal schedule. The AI asks the right qualifying questions to determine urgency and routes accordingly.
Can the system send maintenance reminders to existing customers?
Yes. The system tracks each customer's service history and automatically sends maintenance reminders based on their last service date, equipment type, and your recommended maintenance schedule. Spring AC tune-ups, fall furnace checks, annual plumbing inspections -- the system sends personalized reminders with one-click booking links. Customers who have maintenance agreements get priority scheduling and reminders.
How does review collection work for home services?
After every completed service call, the system sends a text message with a direct link to leave a Google review. The timing is key -- we send it within 2 hours of job completion when the customer is happiest. If they do not respond, a follow-up goes out 2 days later. The system also monitors your Google Business Profile and alerts you to new reviews so you can respond promptly. HVAC companies using our review system typically see a 50-80% increase in Google reviews within 90 days.
Do you integrate with ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, or Jobber?
Yes. We build on GoHighLevel for CRM and marketing automation, and integrate with popular field service platforms including ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, and FieldEdge through Zapier and direct API connections. We can sync customer data, trigger follow-ups from job completions, and keep your field operations and marketing in sync without requiring you to switch platforms.
How quickly can an HVAC automation system be set up?
A basic AI phone answering and booking system can be live in 1-2 weeks. A full home services automation suite with dispatch integration, seasonal campaigns, maintenance reminders, and review collection typically takes 3-4 weeks. Every project includes a 30-day testing period where we monitor and optimize the system before final handoff.
