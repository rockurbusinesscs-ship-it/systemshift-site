Greensboro's Business Landscape

The Third-Largest City in North Carolina is Growing Fast

Greensboro is not just a mid-sized Southern city anymore. With a population approaching 300,000 and a metro area of over 775,000, the Gate City has evolved into one of the most dynamic business hubs in the Southeast. The city sits at the crossroads of I-85 and I-40, making it one of the most strategically located cities on the East Coast for logistics, distribution, and commerce.

Major employers like Cone Health, the University of North Carolina at Greensboro (UNCG), Volvo Trucks North America, and Honda Aircraft Company anchor the local economy, providing tens of thousands of jobs and creating a massive ecosystem of supporting businesses. From the medical practices that serve Cone Health patients to the logistics firms that operate along the PTI Airport corridor, Greensboro's economy is diverse and resilient.

Key industries driving growth include logistics and distribution -- fueled by the Piedmont Triad International Airport (PTI) and the city's position along major interstate corridors -- healthcare, higher education, advanced manufacturing, and professional services. The downtown revitalization has brought new energy to the city, with coworking spaces, boutique agencies, and tech startups joining the established law firms, accounting practices, and consulting businesses that have called Greensboro home for decades.

Guilford County as a whole hosts over 14,000 small businesses, and that number continues to grow. The Nussbaum Center for Entrepreneurship, UNCG's entrepreneurship programs, and the Greensboro Chamber of Commerce all feed a growing small business ecosystem. But many of these businesses still run on spreadsheets, sticky notes, and manual processes. That is where automation makes the biggest difference.
